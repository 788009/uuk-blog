## Unit 1 - Basics of Economics

1. Scarcity
    1. 有限的资源无法满足无限的需求 → 研究如何分配资源
    2. Choices Trade off 三大基本问题
        - What to produce
        - How to produce
        - For whom to produce
2. Factor of production 生产要素
    - Labor
    - Capital 资本
        -  Physical
        -  Human
    - Natural resources / land
    - Entrepreneurship 企业家精神
3. Opportunity cost
4. Production possibility curves (PPC)
    1. 常为凸函数 (increasing opportunity cost): because some resources are not easily adaptable to alternative uses
    2. 直线: constant opportunity cost
    3. 移动: 4 factors + technology
5. Absolute advantage and comparative advantage
    1. 可能 neither have absolute advantage, 不可能 both

## Unit 2 - Economics Indicators and the Business Cycle

1. Circular-flow diagram
    1. Flow
        1. 所有 input 都归 household 所有
        2. household 将 input 卖给 firm
            - firm 付钱给 household (factor markets): wage, rent, profit, interest
        3. firm 将 input 加工成 output 后卖给 household
            - household 付钱给 firm (product markets)
    2. Leakage and injection
        1. Leakage
            1. Savings
            2. Taxes
            3. Imports
        2. Injection
            1. Investment (loan out)
            2. Government purchases of goods and services
            3. Exports
2. GDP (Gross Domestic Product): the market value of all final goods and services produced within a country in a given period of time.
    1. 使用 market value 计算
    2. 只计算 final goods and services: 避免重复计算的问题
        - Value added
    3. 不计算过去的产出
    4. 在国家领土范围内的产出
    5. 不计入 GDP 的内容
        - Non-market or illegal
        - Nonproduction transaction 如炒股、黄金
        - Transfer payment
    6. 计算 GDP
        1. The expenditures approach: $\text{GDP} (Y) = C + I + G + NX$
            1. Consumer spending ($C$)
            2. Investment ($I$): spending on business capital, residental capital (包括买房), and inventories (库存，生产但未卖出)
            3. Government spending ($G$) (not transfer payment)
            4. Net exports ($NX$): exports - imports
        2. The income approach: GDP($Y$) = Wages + Rent + Interest + Profit
        3. The value-added approach: GDP = Firm's output value - Value of intermediate goods
            - $\text{GDP} = \sum({\text{Value added by each firm}})$
    7. Other related variables
        - Disposable income 可自由支配的收入: income - tax
    8. Real vs. nominal GDP
        1. Nominal GDP: measured in current price 当年价格 / 当年销量
            - $\text{GDP} = \sum (\text{Current year price} \times \text{Current year quantity})$
        2. Real GDP: measured in unchanging price
            - $\text{GDP} = \sum (\text{Base year price} \times \text{Current year quantity})$
            - Real GDP adjusts for inflation
        3. GDP deflator: $\cfrac{\text{Nominal GDP}}{\text{Real GDP}} \times 100$
    9. Real GDP per capita 人均 GDP
        - Real GDP per capita is the best measure of a nation's standard of living
    10. GDP does not value:
        1. Quantity of the environment
        2. Leisure time
        3. Non-market activity
            - e.g. childcare (provided by parent)
        4. Equitable distribution of income
3. Price indexes and inflation
    1. Inflation: an increase in the general price level 物价上涨
        - Hyperinflation: 多数情况由政府超发货币导致
    2. Deflation: a decrease in the general price level 物价下降
    3. Disinflation 减缓物价上涨速度
    4. Winners and losers from inflation
        1. Borrowers are helped, lenders are hurted
            - Interest rate
                1. Nominal interest rate: without a correction for the effects for the effects of inflation 购买力不一定增加
                2. Real interest rate: corrected for the effects for the effects of inflation
                    - Fisher's Effect: Real interest rate = Nominal interest rate - Inflation rate
    5. Consumer price index (CPI): intended to show how the cost of all purchases by a typical urban family has changed over time
        1. Basket: 一段时间内购买的商品和服务
        2. $\text{CPI} = \cfrac{\text{Price of basket in current year}}{\text{Price of basket in base year}} \times 100$
            - 全部使用 base year 的数量
        3. Shortcomings of CPI
            1. Substitution Bias: CPI 不会考虑物价上涨导致消费者转而购买其他商品
            2. Introduction of New Goods
            3. Unmeasured Quality Change: 如 CPI 不会考虑零食净含量的变化
    6. $\text{Inflation rate in year 2} = \cfrac{\text{Price level in year 2} - \text{Price level in year 1}}{\text{Price level in year 1}} \times 100\%$
        其中 Price level 可以是以下标准，计算结果不同
        1. GDP deflator
        2. CPI
    7. Costs of inflation
        1. Shoe-leather cost: 人们倾向于花钱
        2. Menu costs: 重制菜单标注新价格要多花钱
        3. Unit-of-account costs: 钱作为计量单位的功能降低
4. Unemployment
    1. Employed
        1. Worked as paid employees or in their own business
        2. Both full-time and part-time workers
        3. Temporarily absent from work
    2. Unemployed
        1. Not employed
        2. Available for work
        3. Had tried to find employment during the previous four weeks
    3. Not in the labor force
        1. Discouraged workers
        2. Full-time students
        3. Homemakers
        4. Retirees
    4. $\text{Unemployment rate} = \cfrac{\text{Number of the unemployed}}{\text{Labor force}}$
    5. $\text{Labor-force Participation Rate} = \cfrac{\text{Labor force (= Employed + Unemployed)}}{\text{劳动年龄人口}}$
    6. 失业率算不准的原因
        1. Discouraged workers
        2. Under-employed workers: 部分时间失业
        3. Illegal labor: 有工作但不敢上报
    7. Type of unemployment
        1. Frictional unemployment
        2. Structural unemployment: 在特定工资水平下劳动力供不应求
            1. Technology progress
            2. Change in the economic structure
            3. Minimum wage
            4. Labor union
            5. Efficiency wages
        3. Cyclical unemployment: 经济变差导致的失业
    8. Natural unemployment = Frictional unemployment + Structural enployment
5. Business cycle
    1. Recession, trough
    2. Expansion, peak
        1. 物价上升, inflation rate 高
    3. Output gap: the difference between actual output and potential output ($Y_\text{p}$)
        1. Inflationary (positive) gap: $Y > Y_\text{p}$
            - Actual unemployment rate < natural rate of unemployment
            - No cyclical unemployment
        2. Recessionary (negative) gap
            - Actual unemployment rate > natural rate of unemployment

## Unit 3 - National Income and Price Determination

1. Aggregate demand: $C + I + G + NX$
    1. Shape
        1. Price level (PL) ↑, real GDP (Y) ↓
        2. PL ↓, Y ↑
        3. Reasons
            1. The wealth effect: PL ↑ → real wealth (钱的实际价值) ↓ → consumer spending ↓ → Y ↓
            2. The interst-rate effect: PL ↑ → money demand ↑ → interest rate ↑ → investment ↓ → Y ↓
            3. The exchange-rate effect: PL ↑ → money demand ↑ → interest rate ↑ → demand for currency ↑ → appreciate (货币升值) → net export ↓ → Y ↓
    2. Shifters
        1. Consumption
            1. Disposable income (Y - tax)
            2. Income tax
            3. Marginal propensity to consume (MPC) 愿意花掉新获得钱的占比
            4. Expectation and confidence
                1. 对将来经济乐观 → 消费 C 增加 → AD ↑
            5. Wealth (包括尚未变现的资产)
            6. Interest rate (大单价商品)
        2. Investment
            1. Interest rate
            2. Confidence
                1. 对将来经济乐观 → 投资 I 增加 → AD ↑
            3. Business tax
            4. Technology
        3. Government spending
            1. Fiscal policy
        4. Net export
            1. Foreign and domestic GDP or income
            2. Exchange rate
            3. Relative price level
            4. Consumer taste and preference
2. Long-run aggregate suppy
    1. Shape: 竖直直线，无论 PL 是多少，LRAS 均为定值 
    2. Shifters
        1. Factors of production (数量和质量)
        2. Technology
3. Short-run aggregate supply   
    1. Shape: 单调递增
        1. Price level (PL) ↑, real GDP (Y) ↑
        2. PL ↓, Y ↓
        3. Reasons
            1. The sticky-wage theory
                1. Nominal wages are slow to adjust to changing economic condition
                2. PL ↑ → output price ↑ but wages sticky → profitablity ↑ → Y ↑
            2. The sticky-price theory
                1. Menu cost
                2. PL ↑ → flexible price ↑, sticky price 不变 → sticky price profitablity ↑ → Y ↑
    2. Shifters
        1. LRAS shifters
        2. production cost (factors 的价格) 
            1. Nominal wages
            2. Raw material price
4. Macroeconomic equilibrium
    1. AD, SRAS, LRAS 交于一点：短期长期均达到均衡
5. Output gap
    1. Recessionary gap
        1. SRAS 与 AD 的交点 $Y < Y_\text{p}$
        2. Effect
            1. Nominal wages ↓
                1. Labor 需求降低，wages 降低
                2. 常伴随 deflation
            2. Production cost ↓ → SRAS ↑
    2. Inflationary gap
        1. SRAS 与 AD 的交点 $Y > Y_\text{p}$
        2. Effect
            1. Nominal wages ↑
                1. Labor 抢手, wages 水涨船高
                2. 常伴随 inflation, 员工主动要求涨工资
            2. Production cost ↑ → SRAS ↓
6. Self correction of SRAS: In the long run, in the absence of government policy, SRAS 会自行恢复
7. Fiscal policy
    1. Fiscal policy
        1. Government spending
            - 影响 AD
        2. Taxation / transfer payment
            - 影响 disposable income → consumption → AD
    2. Multiplier effect
        1. $\Delta \text{AD} = G \cdot \sum\limits_{i=0}^\infty \text{MPC}^i = G \cdot \cfrac{1}{1 - \text{MPC}}$
        2. Spending multiplier: $\cfrac{1}{1 - \text{MPC}}$ or $\cfrac{1}{\text{MPS}}$
        3. Marginal propensity to save (MPS) = 1 - MPC
        4. Tax multiplier: $-\cfrac{\text{MPC}}{1 - \text{MPC}}$
    3. Lag
        1. Recognition lag: 政府认识到问题时，问题已经持续一段时间了
        2. Administrative lag: 从草案到通过需要时间
        3. Operational lag
    4. Automatic stabilizers
        1. Progressive tax system
            1. GDP ↑ → tax revenues ↑ →  consumption ↓ → prevent overheating
            2. GDP ↓ → tax revenues ↓ →  consumption ↑ → prevent recession
        2. Transfer payment programs: 失业即可向政府领取失业补助
            1. Recession → people qualified for transfer payment programs ↑ → disposable income ↑

## Unit 4 - Financial Sector

1. Financial assets: paper claim
    1. Financial system: providing liquidity 资产变现能力
    2. Bonds 债券: loans that represent debt that the government or a corporation must repay to an investor
        1. The bond holder has no ownership of the company
        2. Present value (PV) → Future value (FV)
            1. $\text{PV} \cdot (1 + r)^n = \text{FV}$
            2. $\text{PV} = \cfrac{\text{FV}}{(1 + r)^n}$
            3. 相同数额的钱，现在价值一定比将来价值高
        3. US government bonds / treasury bills 世界公认风险最低
            1. Commercial bank 大量持有 government bonds
            2. 日本是美国政府最大的债主
    3. Stocks 股票
        1. Stockowners have ownership of the company
        2. Profit
            1. Dividends 分红，股息
            2. A capital gain 低买高卖的差价
2. Money
    1. Money is any asset that is accepted as a means of payment
    2. Barter economy 以物易物
        1. 缺陷: double coincidence of wants 无法达成交易
        2. Transaction cost 不方便
    3. Type
        1. Commodity money 商品货币：货币自身有价值
            1. Cigarette
            2. Gold
        2. Fiat money: without intrinsic, government decree 政府需要有公信力
            1. Paper notes
            2. Coins
    4. Function
        1. Medium of exchange
        2. Unit of account
        3. Store of value
        4. Standard of deferred payment
3. Measuring the money supply
    1. M0 (monetary base)
        1. Currency in circulation
        2. Reserves in bank
    2. M1: 最 liquid = money supply
        1. Currency: cash, coins
        2. Demand deposits: 随时可取用，如活期存款 checking account / checkable deposits
            - 无利息
        3. Savings deposits 有利息，不经常取钱
    3. M2
        1. M1
        2. Small-denomination time deposit 小额定期存款
            - Certificate of deposit
        3. Money market mutual funds
4. Bank
    1. Central bank
    2. Commercial bank
        1. Balance sheet
            1. Asset: 钱的去处
                1. Reserve: 银行存在金库里的钱，应对储户取钱
                    - 放贷时减少
                2. Loans: 已经放出去的贷
                    - 放贷时增加
                3. Securities 证券
            2. Liabilities: 钱的来源

            |Asset|Liabilities (& owner's equity)|
            |---|---|
            |Reserve \$200|Demand deposit \$800|
            |Loans \$100|Debt \$150|
            |Securities \$100|Owner's equity \$50|
        2. Money multiplying
            1. Reserve ratio & money multiplier: 若每家银行拿到存款都贷 90\% 出去，而每个人拿到贷款之后都存 90\% 到下一家银行，则 money supply 会变成原来的 10 倍
                1. Reserve ratio: 存进金库的比例, $\cfrac{\text{Reserve}}{\text{Demand deposit}}$
                    - Required reserve ratio / reserve requirement
                    - $\text{Required reserve} = \text{Required reserve ratio} \times \text{Demand deposit}$
                    - Excess reserve = Total reserve - Required reserve
                    - Maximum amount of additional loans = Reserve - Required reserve
                2. Money multiplier: $\cfrac{1}{\text{Reserve ratio}}$
5. Money market
    1. Demand for money (money demand, MD)
        1. Transaction demand: everyday purchases
        2. Asset demand: 存钱
    2. Interst rate and the quantity demanded for money: inverse relationship
        1. Opportunity cost of holding money
        2. Shape: downward
        3. Shift of MD
            1. Price level ↑ → MD ↑
            2. Income or real GDP ↑ → consumption ↑ → MD ↑
    3. Money supply (MS)
        1. Shape: vertical
            - Fixed by the central bank
        2. Shift: monetary policy
    4. 图像
        1. 横轴: Quantity of money
        2. 纵轴: Nominal interest rate
        3. 线: MS, MD
6. 银行经济情况
    1. Discount rate: the interst rate that central bank charges commercial banks on its loans to them
    2. Policy rate (federal funds rate): the interst rate that commercial banks charge one another on their overnight loans
    3. Interest on reserve: the interst rate that central bank pays command banks on their reserves at the central bank
    4. Policy rate 在 interest of reserve 和 discount rate 之间时，commercial banks 才愿意与其他 commercial banks 交易
    5. Supply: 由 central bank 决定，不受 policy rate 影响
7. Monetary policy
    1. Limited reserves: money supply from central bank 会影响 demand
        1. Open market operations: the purchase and sale of US government bonds
            1. Central bank (CB) 把市场上的债券买回 → CB 的资金流入市场 → MS ↑ → interst rate ↑ → investment ↑ → AD ↑ → GDP ↑
            2. CB 卖债券 → CB 把市场中的资金收回 → MS ↓ → ... → AD ↓
        2. Reserve requirement
            1. Reserve ratio ↑ → MS ↓ → ... → AD ↓
            2. Reserve ratio ↓ → MS ↑ → ... → AD ↑
        3. Discount rate
            1. Discount rate ↑ → 央行不想借钱给银行，银行向央行借钱成本上升 → MS ↓
    2. Ample reserve
        1. Administered interst rate → policy rate → interest rate → investment → AD
        2. Open market operation: only to maintain ample reserve
8. Loanable funds market 可贷资金市场
    1. Supply: saving
        1. Private saving
        2. Public saving (government)
    2. Demand: borrowing
        1. Government
        2. Investment
    3. 图像
        1. 横轴: quantity of loanable funds
        2. 纵轴: real interest rate
        3. 线: demand, supply
    4. Crowding out effect (fiscal policy)
        1. Government spending ↑ or tax ↓ → borrowing ↑ → demand for loanable funds ↑ → interst rate ↑ → investment ↓
        2. Government spending ↑ or tax ↓ → public savings ↓ → supply for loanable funds ↓ → interst rate ↑ → investment ↓

## Unit 5 - Long-Run Consequences of Stabilization Policies

1. The Phillips Curve
    1. 横轴: Unemployment rate (\%)
        - 与 price level 变化方向相同
    2. 纵轴: Inflation rate (\%)
        - 与 real GDP 变化方向相反
    3. 线
        1. Short-run Phillips Curve (SRPC): 递减
        2. Long-run Phillips Curve (LRPC) = natural rate of unemployment ($u_N$)
    4. Shift
        1. AD 线动, SRPC 点动
        2. SRAS 线动, SRPC 线动
        3. LRAS 线动，LRPC 线动
2. Expected inflation
    1. Actual inflation > expected inflation: inflation gap
    2. Actual inflation < expected inflation: recession gap
    3. Unemployment rate = Natural rate of unemployment - a * (Actual inflation - Expeed inflation)
3. Quantity theory of money
    1. $\text{Velocity} = \cfrac{\text{Nominal GDP}}{\text{Money supply}}$
    2. $V = \cfrac{P \times Y}{M} \Leftrightarrow M \times V = P \times Y$
        - V: Velosity
        - M: money supply
        - P: Price level
        - Y: real GDP
    3. 大规模通货膨胀来自超发货币
4. Government deficit
5. Economic growth
    1. Economic growth 用 real GDP (per capita) 衡量
    2. Standard of living 用 real GDP per capita 衡量
    3. Productivity 用 real GDP per capita 衡量
    4. Factors
        1. Labor: 属于 capita，人越多 per capita 越低
        2. Human capital, physical capital, resources + technical advancements 与 real GDP per capita 正相关
    5. Aggregate production function: $Y = F(K, L)$
        - K: capital
        - L: labor
    6. Policy examples
        1. Encourage saving and investment
        2. Encourage investment from abroad
        3. Encourage education and training
        4. Establish secure property rights and maintain political stability
        5. Promote free trade
        6. Control population growth
        7. Promote research and development

## Unit 6 - Open Economy—International Trade and Finance

1. Trade balance
    1. Trade surplus: exports > imports
    2. Trade dificit: imports < exports
    3. Balanced trade: exports = imports
2. Balance of paymant (BOP): 记账系统
    1. Accounts
        1. Current account (CA): 收入- 支出
            1. Net exports (goods + services): exports - imports
            2. Net income from abroad: income earned from foreigners - income payment made to foreigners
                1. Wage
                2. Rent
                3. Interst
                4. Dividends 股息
            3. Net unilateral transfers: transfer from foreigners - transfer to foreigners
        2. Capital and financial account (CFA): foreign purchase of domestic assets (mmoney in) - domestic purchase of foreign assets (money out)
            1. Assets
                1. Financial assets / foreign direct investment: stocks, bonds
                2. Capital / foreign portfolio investment: firms, factories, land, port
    2. Credit & debit 收支
    3. Net balance of payment: CA balance + CFA balance = 0
3. Exchange rate
    1. Nominal exchange rate
    2. Real exchange rate: 按 goods and services 计算
    3. 图像
        1. 横轴: quantity of domestic currency
        2. 纵轴: foreign currency / domestic currency
    4. Demand for a currency: goods, services, financial assets 买美元的人
        1. 需要一个国家的出口，就需要这个国家的货币
            1. 对出口的需求越多，demand for currency 越多，该国货币将 appreciate 升值
        2. Interest rates / return on asset (bonds): come from the loanable funds market or the money market
            1. Interest rate 越高，人们越想买这个国家的债券，于是 demand for currency 越多，appreciate
    5. Supply: 卖美元的人
        1. 从国外进口需要把自己的货币卖给别人，supply for currency 增加，depreciate
        2. Interest rate / return on asset (bonds) 下降，人们将卖出货币买其他国家的债券，supply for currency 增加，depreciate
    6. Shifters
        1. Inflation rate
            1. Domestic price level ↑ → 外国人认为从本国进口变贵 → 本国 export ↓ → demand for currency ↓ → depreciate
            2. Foreign price level ↑ → 本国人认为从外国进口变贵 → 本国 import ↓ → supply for currency ↓ appreciate
        2. Interest rate
            1. Domestic interest rate ↑ → return on domestic asset ↑ → demand for currency ↑ → appreciate
            2. Foreign interst rate ↑ → return on foreign asset ↑ → supply for currency ↑ → depreciate
        3. Income
            1. Domestic income ↑ → import ↑ → supply ↑ → depreciate
            2. Foreign income ↑ → export ↑ → demand ↑ → appreciate
    7. Appreciate → 外国人兑换货币变贵 → 外国人认为本国物价变贵 → 本国 export ↓
        1. 升值有利于进口，贬值有利于出口
